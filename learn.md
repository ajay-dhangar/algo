# Learn with Algo

Welcome to **Learn with Algo**! Whether you are a beginner taking your first steps into programming or an experienced developer brushing up on complex computer science concepts, you are in the right place. 

Algorithms and Data Structures (DSA) are the foundational building blocks of efficient software development. Understanding them doesn't just help you clear technical interviews—it transforms the way you approach problem-solving.

## Your Learning Roadmap

To make your journey smooth and structured, we have divided the core computer science concepts into clear, progressive tracks. Each track focuses on a specific area of DSA, starting from the basics and moving towards more advanced topics. Here's what you can expect:

### 1. The Fundamentals

Before diving into complex logic, build a rock-solid foundation:

* **Time & Space Complexity:** Master Big O notation to analyze and optimize your code.
* **Basic Mathematics for Algo:** Prime numbers, GCD, LCM, and modular arithmetic.
* **Bit Manipulation:** Understand binary representations and bitwise operations.

### 2. Core Data Structures (The Building Blocks)

Learn how data is organized, managed, and stored efficiently:
* **Arrays & Strings:** Continuous memory allocation, two-pointer techniques, and sliding windows.
* **Linked Lists:** Singly, doubly, and circular linked lists.
* **Stacks & Queues:** LIFO and FIFO principles, and their real-world applications.
* **Hashing / Hash Tables:** Fast lookups, collision resolution techniques, and key-value mapping.

### 3. Advanced Data Structures

Take your data management skills to the next level:
* **Trees:** Binary Trees, Binary Search Trees (BST), AVL Trees, and Tries.
* **Graphs:** Adjacency lists/matrices, representations, and connectivity.
* **Heaps:** Max-Heap, Min-Heap, and priority queues.

### 4. Algorithmic Paradigms (The Techniques)

Learn the design patterns used to solve complex computing problems:
* **Searching & Sorting:** Binary Search, Quick Sort, Merge Sort, and Heap Sort.
* **Recursion & Backtracking:** Thinking recursively, solving Mazes, N-Queens, and Sudoku.
* **Divide and Conquer:** Breaking problems down into manageable sub-problems.
* **Greedy Method:** Making locally optimal choices at each step.
* **Dynamic Programming (DP):** Overlapping subproblems, memoization vs. tabulation, and classic DP problems.
* **Graph Algorithms:** Breadth-First Search (BFS), Depth-First Search (DFS), Dijkstra's, Kruskal's, and Prim's algorithms.
